What we did in Iceland

And maybe you should think about doing when you're there too

We stayed in Reykjavik so most of these are in and around the city.

Attractions

  • Reykjavik

    The town is really compact and you can see most of it on foot.

    There's a nice lake/pond in the middle of the city you can wander around and the shops are located predominantly on two main streets.

    We didn't make it there, but you might be interested in checking out The Icelandic Phallalogical Museum.

  • Hallgrimskirkja

    Striking concrete cathedral in the city. Small fee to climb up to the roof for good views.

  • Perlan

    We walked but it's a decent trek. Revolving fine dining if you fancy it but we went during the day and had a sweet snack in the cafeteria.

  • Harpa Concert Hall and Conference Centre

    Pretty seaside building, you can wander around or catch a performance. Not far from The Sun Voyager sculpture.

  • Icelandic Horse Park - Fákasel

    Part of a package bus tour to see the Golden Circle: see some horses, drink some coffee, buy some expensive souvenirs.

  • Geysir

    Part of a package bus tour to see the Golden Circle: watch the ever reliable Strokkur spurt every 8 minutes. We had lunch in the adjoining visitor centre which was actually pretty decent despite catering for all those tourists.

  • Gullfoss

    Part of a package bus tour to see the Golden Circle: awesome waterfall, loads of tourists.

  • Thingvellir

    Yeh that Þ is pronounced as "th"! Part of a package bus tour to see the Golden Circle: cool national park where parliament started, have a wander and feel like you're in Game of Thrones.

  • Laugarvatn Fontana

    Part of a package bus tour to see the Golden Circle: where we went for a thermal spa dip, avoiding the hordes at the Blue Lagoon. You might be able to try some rye bread baked in the ground.

Food

  • Restó

    Quite dear but food really good.

  • Sægreifinn

    Here you can have the famous fermented shark if you so desire. We settled for fish kebabs and they were scrummy.

  • Icelandic Fish and Chips

    Very popular, decent but nothing amazing.

  • The Laundromat Cafe

    Great place for brunch, gets quite busy. Excellent portion sizes. Try the "clean brunch".

  • Messinn

    Where we had our last supper in Reykjavik, a very nice way to end the trip. Good seafood.

  • Places we missed

    Sandholt was a highly rated bakery/cafe that we didn't stop by at.

    We also saw good things about Bæjarins Beztu Pylsur if you fancy a hot dog.

Drinks

  • Reykjavik Roasters

    The hipster's choice. Beard optional.

  • Kaffitar

    Multiple locations, Iceland's (nicer) version of Costa.

  • Hurra

    We came here for a pre-dinner drink. Or it could have been the place next door, Gaukurinn, because they look pretty similar. I think there was live music. Standing room only and loud, pretty nice vibe.

  • Bar at Hotel Reykjavik Centrum

    This is the hotel we stayed at. The bar inside was cosy, did snacks as well as cocktails using Brennivín.
